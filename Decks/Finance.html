I want to lead in to our summary by saying that 2023 is huge year in W3C history; a major changeover year. We used to invoice and collect dues independently in each host; we have centralized these function and are nearly complete in transitioning to collecting dues centrally now - with the caveat that W3C Beihang members are now being invoiced in a common manner, but the invoicing and payments continued to be processed by Beihang for the time being. Likewise, budgeting used to be managed inside each Host, with only some coordination across them; now, budgeting will now be done through the Board, with coordination with the Partners. The 2023 budget is a composite of Partner planning, but rolled up into one common model. The 2024 budget will go through Board approval in its entirety.

Significant changes in 2023: Centralizing our Finances

  • Invoicing and Collection was done by Host separately, now centralized (except for China)
  • Budgeting was by each Host separately with some coordination, now centralized through the Board with Partner coordination
One of the concerns that we had, as some of you may remember from the end of last year, was that deferred revenue might not be properly transitioned. Since members are billed ahead of when membership is provided, those dues are accrued, and in taking over the member contracts, W3C Inc takes over the responsibility for serving those members. If this already-collected, deferred revenue were not transferred to the new organization, it would have made us very tight on cash. I am pleased to report that we have addressed this concern: we had calculated that the value of transferred member contracts from MIT was approximately $1.55M, and as of April 1, MIT has transferred over $1.84M in cash to account for that. We can clearly claim financial success here; Keio has also agreed to a calculation of accruals, and is transferring or using those funds to pay for 2023 costs, as appropriate. We are still working with ERCIM, but expect a similar arrangement; Beihang will continue as is for now. In addition, I’m pleased to report that we are maintaining a good cash balance; we currently have over $2M in the bank, so we have good liquidity and are not currently concerned about cash flow.

Accruals Transferred to W3C, Inc. in 2023

  • MIT: $1.55M accrued, $1.84M received through April 1, 2023
  • Keio: $404K accrued, transferring or applying to 2023 costs
  • ERCIM: not final, arrangement similar to MIT/Keio expected
  • Beihang: continuing operation

W3C Inc has $2.2M in the bank - we have good liquidity

Having allayed any immediate fears, I want to step back and talk about the finance committee's three main areas of focus - first, we need to stabilize our operations, which has been a process, definitely with a learning curve. We have initially focused on making sure we have solid procedures for revenue collection, and are executing on billing and the like; including ensuring we are properly mitigating risks, for example by using FDIC-insuring banking. Of course all of this is built on the foundation I just discussed, that we are consolidating our financial planning and budgeting from our old multi-Host model to the new central W3C Inc with partners. The second area of focus is implementing the use of best practices in our accounting and operations, in order to properly oversee the financial status of the organization (by both the Board and members). This requires us to implement standard accounting procedures, and ensure we are using common best practices for record keeping, standard accounting methods, and standard reporting formats. We have done some of this, but expect to spend the remainder of 2023 working on this, at least. Finally, the Finance Committee will need to focus on long-term strategic financial planning - but I want to set the expectations that this is not the finance committee's focus right now. We will talk more about that later.

Areas of Focus

So let's talk about where exactly we do stand. Our Q1 numbers have not been vetted yet, but we are running under one quarter of our overall budget for the year in the first quarter, as we should. I want to caution that the books for Q1 are not closed and vetted yet, and some expenses will hit harder later in the year; so this is not a full cash flow projection. In other actions, the invoices that were in arrears are largely cleaned up, and our invoicing is proceeding well.

Where do we stand?

  • Preliminary Q1 numbers are running under 1/4 of W3C Inc. budget
    • Q1 books not completely closed and vetted yet
    • We have some open positions
    • Under budget in some areas that may change
  • Invoicing for member dues is in place
    • Q1 was billed later than we’d like, Q2 is better
    • Virtual is invoicing all members outside China

Looking ahead, the cleaning up of past-due invoices suggested we need a clearer member non-payment termination policy; we expect to have that ready to go very soon. We need to define and implement Key Performance Indicators to use to analyze and plan; We will ramp up our FY 2024 Budget planning in order to have it approved at the October Board meeting. Fundamentally, we expect much of our work is in implementing best practices and preparing for audit readiness, as well as the annual 990 filings we need for non-profit status. We will be planning an independent audit of our Financial Statements.
