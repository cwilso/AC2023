CEO search

What we've done

Chartered the Personnel Committee in November 2022

Chairs: Léonie Watson, Dom Hazaël-Massieux

Members: Eric Siow, Mark Nottingham, Hongru Zhu, Gonzalo Camarillo

Regular attendees: David Singer, Ralph Swick, Bill Judge

Invited guests: Sheila Moussavi

Personnel Committee charter amended
"The Committee is delegated to make decisions pertaining to the search for the CEO"
Including:
  • Select a search agency
  • Approve process and timeline
  • Approve CEO profile and job description
  • Identify shortlist of candidates

Issued Request For Proposals

Issued in January 2023, to gather information to inform the Board's decision to use a search agency or not

In February 2023, the Board decided to use a search agency as an investment in the future of W3C

  • Dedicated team
  • Experience and expertise
  • Efficient process
  • International presence

Selected an agency

5 proposals evaluated using 18 criteria

2 agencies selected for interview, and asked 9 specific questions

Perrett Laver (PL) was chosen because of experience with Standards Development Organizations (SDO) like ISOC and IETF, non-profits, and organizations with multi-stakeholder communities

Initial meeting held on 10 March 2023

Conducted a stakeholder consultation

Perrett Laver conducted a consultation with representatives from the W3C community

Meetings with Directors, Officers, senior Team members, Advisory Board (AB) and Technical Architecture Group (TAG) Chairs

A town hall with the entire Team

Surveys of the Advisory Committee (AC), and Working Group (WG) and Interest Group (IG) Chairs

Where we are now

"The next CEO of W3C will be a strategic, collaborative, and motivating leader.

They will have a global mindset and the ability to effectively engage with a diverse range of stakeholders.

They will be a person of integrity who is forward thinking and supportive of staff.

Most importantly, they will have an affinity for, and understanding of, the work, mission, and values of W3C."

Where we go from here

12 May: Closing date for applications

W/C 15 May: Perrett Laver provides Personnel Committee with a longlist of candidates

22 May: Personnel Committee and Perrett Laver review the candidate longlist

23 to 29 May: Perrett Laver interviews longlist candidates

Identify the shortlist

W/C 5 June: Perrett Laver provides Personnel Committee with a shortlist of candidates

W/C 12 June: Personnel Committee and Perrett Laver review the candidate shortlist

W/C 19 or 26 June: Personnel Committee and Perrett Laver interview shortlist candidates

The final steps

Early July: Personnel Committee recommends candidates for final interview

Late July/early August: Final interviews conducted by an interview panel of six to eight Directors, in Wakefield MA

Early August: Interview panel recommends a candidate for approval by the Directors, and on approval an offer is made

End game

By the time we meet again at TPAC, we hope to be able to introduce the next W3C CEO to everyone
