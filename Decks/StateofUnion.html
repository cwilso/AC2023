The W3C members name representatives to the advisory committee (AC), elect to the TAG and AB, and appoint to working groups. The AC approves charters (and document progression) The AB oversees the governing Process document The TAG defines architectural principles and oversees technical integrity Work groups produce our primary product: Recommendations and the Team supports the entire organization.
Not shown: formal objections and escalation

Technical Governance Review

The Members appoint to the AC, who elect the AB and TAG, appoint to WGs, who produce RECs

David: What does governance look like now and into the future?
A short phrase we have used is to say we are becoming more member-led: that's you - that's Us!
previously the members were not much involved in the formal governance side
One aspect of incorporation is that we HAVE a Board of Directors, with formal authority over governance: legal, money, and operational matters
The members elect the largest class to the Board of Directors
the Partners also appoint, and there is provision for Board-appointed seats
The Board oversees the CEO directly, and coordinates the Partners
Under the Partnership model, the Partners continue to employ most of the team
the team is more closely coordinated as a single world-wide team under the CEO
This is what we're becoming: a consortium where the members (on one side) and the staff (on the other) work together to lead us – lead the web – forward

Governance as it is (becoming)

Member-led, elected Board that oversees, Partners also appoint to the Board

Ralph: We've mentioned this conceptual change from Host to Partner
But what does it mean?
As we already mentioned, our 'founding Host', MIT, has for years been encouraging us to progress forward from being one of MIT's research projects
MIT launched W3C, with some help, and provided us a stable home for nearly 3 decades and for that we owe them a huge debt of gratitude. MIT still houses a vital part of our Web Accessibility work but has decided not to continue as a formal Partner.
In Japan there is a new Foundation that is now the formal Partner; that foundation continues to work with Keio University while some of the team remain at the University
We have interim partner agreements with ERCIM and Beihang while the precise details of their Partner agreement are in the process of resolution
One key change in these new arrangements is that the Board has overall budgetary control,
and with the exception of China, we are collecting member dues centrally
and coordinating their expenditure globally
